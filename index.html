THE STOCKING VERSUS THE VOLCANO

Calzone, pronounced kal-zoh-nay, in Italian means “one pant leg” or “stocking”. Stromboli is named after the 1950 Roberto Rossellini/Ingrid Bergman movie, which in turn is named after the movie’s setting on the volcanic island of Stromboli. A Calzone is a pocket of dough filled with cheese and a topping and baked brown. It is served smothered in tomato sauce. A Stromboli is more of a sandwich roll with dough rolled around meat, cheese and sauce. It is served sliced after the cheese cools a bit.

Phillies

Developed in the 1930s in Philadelphia as chopped W steak served on a hoagie roll, history was made when onions were added and topped with Provolone cheese. The term hoagie originated in￼the Philadelphia area. Italians working at the World War I–era shipyard, known as Hog Island, introduced the sandwich, by putting various meats, cheeses, and lettuce between two slices of bread. This became known as the "Hog Island" sandwich; shortened to Hoggies, then the "hoagie". All oven toasted and in half/whole.

Not Just Bread
and Meat

The first written usage of the English word "Sandwich" appeared in 1762. It was named after John Montagu. It is said that he ordered his valet to bring him meat tucked between two pieces of bread, and because Montagu also happened to be the Fourth Earl of Sandwich, others began to order "the same as Sandwich!" It is said that Lord Sandwich was fond of this form of food because it allowed him to continue playing cards, particularly cribbage, while eating without getting his cards greasy. Before being known as sandwiches, it was simply known as "bread and meat".

NOTES ON REHEATING OUR PIZZA

Please do not use a microwave to reheat our pizza! The best way to reheat pizza slices is in a frying pan. It takes longer, but it is worth it. What happens when you nuke a piece of pizza in the microwave is that you excite the food and water molecules which vibrate and generate heat. The water molecules turns into steam and what you get is dry tough pizza. If you put your stove burner on medium-low and put your slice in a frying pan, you heat your pizza gently. Shake it every now and then to keep it from sticking and the crust will get nice and crispy and the cheese and toppings will slowly begin to warm. When the cheese on top is pretty warm, the slice is done. If the bottom gets too brown, the heat is too high. That’s all there is to it, and the pie will taste as good as when you ordered it.

STOCKING VERSUS THE VOLCANO

Calzone, pronounced kal-zoh-nay, in Italian means “one pant leg” or “stocking”. Stromboli is named after the 1950 Roberto Rossellini/Ingrid Bergman movie, which in turn is named after the movie’s setting on the volcanic island of Stromboli. A Calzone is a pocket of dough filled with cheese and a topping and baked brown. It is served smothered in tomato sauce. A Stromboli is more of a sandwich roll with dough rolled around meat, cheese and sauce. It is served sliced after the cheese cools a bit.

CALZONE 8.50

Ricotta and mozzarella stuffed into half-moon shaped dough. Add one regular topping of your choice or go with Genoa salami. Brushed with cream and baked brown and served with a slathering of red sauce.

Dough pressed thin with toppings, sauce and cheese rolled up. It is baked golden with slashes in the top to let out the steam. It is then cooled and cut into slices. Our two regular toppings are ham and pepperoni, but you can also choose your own two regular toppings.
